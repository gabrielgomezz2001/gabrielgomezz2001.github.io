What it means This was Bam's best version of himself. With Ware anchoring the rim, Bam got to operate as a face-up four, posting career-high efficiency from three (41.8%) and pushing his scoring near 23 a night. The numbers say it is the right direction long term, but the team needs to commit to a true backup center to make these minutes sustainable.

Small-ball logic Against teams that play four shooters and a mobile center (Boston, Indiana, OKC), the small-ball five lets Wiggins switch one through four and keeps Bam at the rim. Mitchell sets the table, Powell and Herro stretch the floor, and the offense gets to play in transition off the league's fastest pace.
Big-ball logic Against teams with two real bigs (Cleveland, Memphis, Minnesota, Denver), going big with Bam at the 4 and Ware at the 5 matches the size up front and gives you the lineup that posted Bam's most efficient scoring stretch of the year. Wiggins slides to the 3 where his defensive versatility is wasted on smaller teams but valuable against physical wings.
Why these specifically Both lineups keep Mitchell and Powell as the backcourt because that pairing was the team's most consistent two-way duo. The swap is at the 3, 4, and 5. The data says Bam plays better at the 4, Ware was a +3.6 on/off, and Wiggins was meaningfully better at the 4 than the 3, which means the only real question is how much you trust Herro defensively against a given matchup.
