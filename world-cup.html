How this works (kinda)

What's an Elo rating? Just a number that says how good a team is. Originally invented for chess ratings back in the 60s, then borrowed by soccer. Higher number = stronger team. Spain has 2171. Qatar has 1450. The bigger the gap, the more lopsided the matchup. When two teams play, the result updates both of their ratings (winner gains points, loser drops).

How the score gets predicted. First, the Elo gap between the two teams sets how many goals each one is expected to score on average. Stronger team gets more expected goals. Then I roll random numbers based on those averages. Sometimes Argentina scores 4 against Qatar. Sometimes 2. But on average across many matches, it's around what their Elo predicts.

Star players: When a big name is out, their team's Elo drops. Messi out drops Argentina a lot. Ronaldo out drops Portugal less because their squad is deeper. Late sub means half the impact.

Full time vs extra time vs penalties. Full time is the regular 90 minutes. If a knockout match is tied after that, it goes to 30 more minutes (extra time). If still tied, penalty shootout. The probability you see at the top of the result tells you the chances of each outcome happening in regulation. Penalty shootouts are roughly a coin flip, with a tiny edge to the slightly stronger team.

Same matchup run twice gives different results because of randomness. That's kind of the point. Run it 1,000 times in the predictor tab to see the real chances.

All three prediction tabs (this one, the Bracket, and Champion Odds) use the same simulation. Hit run once. Then click between the tabs to explore the data.

How does the group stage work? Tap to read ▾

The basics. 48 teams. 12 groups of 4. Each team plays the other 3 in their group, so every team gets 3 matches. Win = 3 points. Draw = 1 point. Loss = 0.

Who advances? Top 2 from every group go through automatically (that's 24 teams). Then the 8 best 3rd-place teams across all 12 groups also advance. So 32 teams total move to the Round of 32. The 4 worst 3rd-place teams and all 12 last-place teams go home.

If two teams are tied on points. FIFA goes down this list until someone wins:

  1. Head-to-head result (who won when they played each other)
  2. Head-to-head goal difference
  3. Head-to-head goals scored
  4. Goal difference across all group games
  5. Most goals scored across all group games
  6. Fewest yellow and red cards (fair play score)
  7. FIFA world ranking before the tournament

For the 8 best 3rd-place teams across different groups, FIFA can't use head-to-head (they didn't play each other), so they rank them by: points → goal difference → goals scored → fair play → FIFA ranking.

In this predictor I use a simplified version (points → goal difference → goals scored) for the tiebreakers. Real FIFA might break a tie differently in some edge cases, but for the simulations it barely matters.
